Computer Science (CS) is the study of computation, information, and automation. It is a discipline that spans theory and practical application, providing the foundational knowledge to solve complex problems across every industry imaginable.

As BSCS students, we are focused on four primary pillars:

  • Algorithms and Theory: Understanding the mathematical and logical principles that govern efficient problem-solving. This includes the rigorous study of data structures and algorithm design to build solutions that scale.
  • Software Development: Mastering the practice of writing, debugging, and maintaining executable code across various languages (e.g., Python, Java, C++). This skill transforms theory into tangible technology.
  • Data Analysis and Systems: Learning how to store, manage, secure, and extract insights from large datasets, and understanding the architecture of computer systems (hardware, networks, databases).
  • Innovation and Application: Applying these foundations to create new technologies, whether itâ€™s building a modern web application, developing artificial intelligence, or ensuring cybersecurity.

By committing to these pillars, BSCS 102 prepares us to be not just coders, but thoughtful Computational Thinkers capable of designing the next generation of solutions.
